## Kerassentials Ingredients: What's In the Bottle

This is where most reviews just paste a list. Let's actually look at what these ingredients do and why they were chosen together.

### Tea Tree Oil
Probably the most research-backed natural antifungal on the market. The active compound terpinen-4-ol disrupts fungal cell membranes, making it hard for the organism to survive or reproduce. A 2017 study published in *International Journal of Pharmacognosy and Phytochemical Research* evaluated tea tree oil specifically on onychomycosis and found meaningful antifungal activity.

### Lavender Oil
Lavender does double duty: it protects nail keratin (the protein structure of your nails) and has demonstrated antifungal properties in controlled studies. A 2015 study in *Scientifica* found lavender essential oil effective against *Candida albicans*, one of the most common fungal culprits.

### Undecylenic Acid
A fatty acid derived from castor oil. It's been used in FDA-approved antifungal products for decades. Undecylenic acid disrupts the environment fungi need to grow — basically making the nail surface inhospitable. It's one of the few natural compounds with a genuine regulatory track record.

### Aloe Vera
Soothing, anti-inflammatory, and — according to a 2017 study in *Oman Medical Journal* — demonstrably antifungal against pathogenic fungal strains. It also helps the formula absorb into the nail bed rather than sitting on the surface.

### Organic Flaxseed Oil
Acts as a skin-conditioning base. Flaxseed oil is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which support the skin's natural immune response and help with the inflammation often associated with fungal infections.

### Almond Oil
Protective and anti-inflammatory. Helps prevent secondary infections at the nail margin and supports the barrier function of skin around the nail.

### Lemongrass Oil
Efficient antifungal with documented activity against a range of dermatophytes (the fungi that cause most nail infections). Also has mild anti-inflammatory properties.

### Tocopheryl Acetate (Vitamin E)
A stabilized form of Vitamin E. Protects the skin from oxidative damage, supports nail tissue repair, and helps prevent premature aging of the nail bed skin.

**Summary:** The formula combines front-line antifungal actives (tea tree, undecylenic acid, lemongrass) with protective carriers (almond, flaxseed) and recovery agents (aloe, vitamin E). The logic is attack + repair + prevent — rather than just suppression.

## How to Use Kerassentials: Step-by-Step

1. **Clean and dry your nails thoroughly** after bathing or showering. Fungus thrives in moisture — don't trap it under the formula.
2. **Apply the oil using the built-in applicator brush** directly to the nail, nail bed, and surrounding skin.
3. **Gently massage the oil in** for 30–60 seconds to encourage absorption beneath the nail plate.
4. **Repeat four times daily** — typically morning, midday, early evening, and before bed.
5. **Continue for the full recommended course** — the manufacturer suggests 2–6 months depending on infection severity. Don't stop when the nail looks better. Stop when a full healthy nail has grown in.

Consistency is non-negotiable. This is a topical treatment, not a one-time fix.
